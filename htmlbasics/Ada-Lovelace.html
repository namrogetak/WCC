Ada Lovelace

"A new, a vast, and a powerful language is developed for the future use of analysis, in which to wield its truths so that these may become of more speedy and accurate practical application for the purposes of mankind than the means hitherto in our possession have rendered possible."

Ada Lovelace was a 19th Century mathematician and writer who is considered to be the first computer programmer. She worked with inventor and mathematician Charles Babbage on theories for the "Analytical Engine," a proposed mechanical computing device. Lovelace introduced many computing concepts, and she is credited as the first to recognize the Analytical Engine could have applications beyond calculation.

Interesting Facts

  1. She was the daughter of the poet Lord Byron.
  2. At age 12, she wanted to fly, so she investigated bird anatomy and researched different materials to create wings for a book called Flyology.
  3. She studied advanced mathematics with University of London professor Augustus de Morgan.
  4. She has an awesome fictionalized comic about her called The Thrilling Adventures of Lovelace and Babbage by Sydney Padua.
